Connect (CC) campaign — intro & convert

Connect → warm intro → soft pitch (spark a reply) → move to email when they engage → close. Reusable for any campaign.

The setup (2 roles)

Senders = Tannu + Rachna send the connection requests + the warm intro.
Closer / Primary = Milee gives the soft pitch in the group thread; the real conversion happens later, by email/call.

Swap names/accounts for future campaigns — the structure stays the same.

⚠️ Golden rules (protect the accounts)
  • Never write "rent a LinkedIn account" (or anything about the model) in a LinkedIn message. LinkedIn scans for it.
  • Don't paste linkedvelocity.com in a LinkedIn DM — the site reveals the model; the website link goes in the email only.
  • On LinkedIn: stay vague — "scale your outreach." Spark a reply, build rapport, move them off.
  • Token format: use the tool's exact insert buttons — {First Name}, not {firstName} (the wrong format sends literally unfilled).
  • Two senders: keep Milee's pitch generic — "thanks for the intro" (no sender name), so it works whether Tannu or Rachna connected.

1Connection request Tannu / Rachna

Growth-to-growth peer note. Under ~300 characters, genuine, zero pitch → high accept rate.
Message select & copy
Hi [Name] — always keen to connect with other growth folks. Love seeing how people are approaching outbound and acquisition these days. Would be great to have you in my network 👋

2The intro Tannu / Rachna · once connected

Warm, peer-framed handoff to Milee. Generic so it fits either sender.
Message
Hi [Name], thanks for connecting! 🙌 Since we're both in the growth/outbound world, I wanted to quickly introduce you to my colleague Milee — she works with growth and sales teams on scaling their outbound reach, and has helped a bunch of teams get in front of a lot more of their market. Thought it'd be a useful connection. I'll let her take it from here 🙂

3The soft pitch Milee · in the group thread

Names the pain (not the product), ends on a question to spark a reply. No email ask yet — that comes only once they engage (asking upfront feels too forward).
Message
Great to be connected [Name], and thanks for the intro! 🙌 Feels like everyone in the growth world is rethinking their outbound right now — always interesting to hear how different teams are approaching it. The pattern I keep seeing: teams eventually hit a ceiling on LinkedIn — one profile only lets you reach so many people a week, so outbound plateaus no matter how sharp the targeting is. Curious how you're thinking about that at [Company] — has scaling reach been on your mind lately?
💬 When they reply (engaged / interested): then offer the overview — "Happy to send over a quick overview — what's the best email to reach you on?" Now it feels natural, not pushy.

3bLinkedIn follow-up Milee · if no reply in 3–4 days

Message
Hey [Name] — no worries if the timing's off 🙂 If scaling outreach is ever on your radar, happy to share how a few teams are approaching it. Just say the word!

Personalisation tokens

Replace before sending: [Name] = first name · [Company] = their company. In the campaign tool, use the exact insert buttons ({First Name}) — a mistyped token like {firstName} sends unfilled.

💡 The rule of thumb: On LinkedIn — spark a reply, build rapport, move them off. In email — be direct, share the site, close. Ask for the email only after they've engaged, never in the first pitch.
