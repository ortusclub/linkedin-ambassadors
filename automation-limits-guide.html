Safe automation & scraping limits

Per account. LinkedIn changes constantly — so keep automation minimal and stay under these ranges to avoid any disruption.

LinkedIn doesn't publish exact limits, and they shift over time — so these are safe, tried-and-tested ranges that keep you well under the radar. The golden rule: steady and human beats fast and robotic.

⚠️ An honest heads-up: LinkedIn's detection changes day to day — there's no fixed, guaranteed "safe" number, and what's fine one week can shift the next. So our best advice is simple: keep automation to a minimum and stay comfortably below these ranges. The lower and steadier you run, the less chance of any disruption. Treat the numbers below as a ceiling to stay under, not a target to hit.

For data scraping
  • Keep profile views around 100–150/day — a steady pace beats one big burst.
  • Space out searches — don't rip through hundreds of result pages rapidly.
  • Randomize delays between actions (~5–30 seconds) — most tools have this setting; turn it on.
  • Ramp up gradually — run at ~50% for the first 1–2 weeks, then build up.
  • Run during business hours in the account's region — not 24/7.
💡 Sales Navigator gives you more headroom — higher view/search tolerance and no commercial-use limit. But the "steady & human" principle still applies.
Back off immediately if you see
  • A CAPTCHA or security checkpoint
  • A "you've reached your weekly limit" banner
  • A sudden drop in results or acceptance
⚠️ If any of these appear: pause automation for a few days, go manual, then resume slower. This almost always clears it before it becomes a restriction.
Scaling safely

When you want more volume, the safe way isn't cranking one account harder — it's spreading activity across multiple accounts. One account maxed out looks robotic; several at a steady pace look like several real people. That's how you scale reach without the flags.
