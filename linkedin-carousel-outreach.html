Cold outreach

The fastest way to get ignored on LinkedIn?

Make your first message all about you.
LinkedVelocity1 / 6 →
❌ The version I see every day
"Hi Sarah, I'd love to connect and share how we help companies like yours grow. Would you be open to a quick 15 minutes this week?"
Nothing wrong with the grammar. Everything wrong with the aim — it's all about the sender, timed to nothing, and asking for a meeting before there's a reason to meet.

✅ Same outreach, rewritten
"Sarah, saw your team just opened three SDR roles. Scaling outbound and hiring for it at the same time is brutal. Curious how you're splitting the load right now?"

Three things changed
→ It's about her, not us
→ It's tied to something real and current
→ The ask is a genuine question, not a calendar invite
