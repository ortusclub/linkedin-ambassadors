Screening Call · ~15 min

Field marketer — one-call script

One call per applicant. Explain the role, ask the set questions, gauge if they're comfortable → decide the pool. Keep it tight and the same every time.

Step 0 · When they apply — send this first

A light touch to book the call and prep them — keep the model vague (you explain LinkedVelocity live on the call, per Sam). Don't send the website link pre-call.

MessageHi [Name], thanks for applying to our promo / street team role! 🙌 Next step is a quick 15-min call to walk you through the role and answer your questions. Grab a slot here: [booking link] Quick context: • On-the-ground promo — handing out flyers & talking to people at Market! Market!, BGC • ₱2,000/day + ₱500 per successful sign-up • Flexible schedule, no experience needed — we'll train you We'll explain exactly how it works and what you'll be promoting on the call. Talk soon!
Need a Calendly (or similar) booking link so applicants self-schedule — essential at volume. The full explanation stays on the call; the website / role brief is a post-call resource for those who move forward.
Before you dial
  • Record it. Use Fathom (free) — unlimited recording + transcript, no card needed. Drop the recording link in the sheet after.
  • Have the sheet open — jot the scorecard + recording link as you go.
  • Target ~15 min. Same flow every call = faster + easy for Sam to review from the recording.

1 · Open 1 min

Say"Hi [Name], thanks for hopping on! This is a quick chat — I'll walk you through what the role actually is, ask you a few questions, and you can ask me anything. Cool if I record it just for our notes? 👍"

2 · Explain what it is 4 min

Say"So — think of LinkedIn as Facebook, but for professionals. It's where businesses find clients. The catch is LinkedIn limits how many people you can reach from one account, so businesses hit a wall.

That's what we solve. We connect businesses that want more reach with everyday people who have a LinkedIn account they barely use — the business grows, and the account owner gets paid monthly for it. We call those owners our ‘ambassadors.’

Your role would be finding those ambassadors — out on the ground at Market! Market!, handing out flyers, talking to people, and helping the interested ones sign up. That's really it."
Pay"Pay is ₱2,000 a day, plus ₱500 for every person who signs up — uncapped, so the more you bring in, the more you earn. Training's free."

3 · The honesty check 2 min

This is the key gate — are they comfortable with the model? Bring it up yourself so it's out in the open.
Say"One thing I'll be upfront about: technically, sharing a LinkedIn account isn't allowed under LinkedIn's terms — it's a grey area, like a lot of growth tools, but it's not illegal and no one gets scammed. It's always with the owner's consent, they stay in control, and we only work with legitimate businesses. How do you feel about that?"
✅ Watch their reaction. Comfortable / unfazed = good. Clearly uneasy or judgmental after the honest explanation = probably not a fit, and that's fine.

4 · The set questions 6 min

Ask all six, same order every call. Listen for the signal noted under each.

  1. Tell me a bit about yourself — what are you up to these days?Signal: clear communicator? easy to talk to?
  2. Have you done any promo, sales, or people-facing work before?Signal: not required — just gauge comfort. "No" is fine if they're outgoing.
  3. How comfortable are you walking up to strangers and starting a conversation?Signal: THE core skill. Confident vs shy.
  4. What made you interested in this role?Signal: genuine interest in the work vs. only the money.
  5. Are you free for the trial day at Market! Market! (end of July), and onsite work generally?Signal: availability + serious commitment.
  6. If someone brushes you off or says no, how do you handle it?Signal: resilience — street promo gets a lot of no's.

5 · Close 1 min

Say"That's everything from me — any questions? … Great, thanks [Name]! We're speaking with a few people this week and I'll be in touch about next steps, including the trial day. Appreciate your time!"
